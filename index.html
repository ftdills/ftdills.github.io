About

Kiteboarding is an on and above water sport combining aspects of wakeboarding, snowboarding, windsurfing, surfing, paragliding, skateboarding and sailing into one extreme sport. A kiteboarder harnesses the power of the wind with a large controllable power kite to be propelled across the water on a kiteboard similar to a wakeboard or a small surfboard, with or without footstraps or bindings. Kitesurfing is a style of kiteboarding specific to wave riding, which uses standard surfboards or boards shaped specifically for the purpose.

Mentoring

Power Kites

There are so many ways of using the pulling and lifting power of a kite. Skateboards, Snowboards, Surfboards, you name it. It takes a good bit of time to master the art of manipulating the kite in your favour, but the retunr on the time invested is overwhealming.

Four-line Kite Flying

Four line kites are designed for the pilots who like mastering acrobatic tricks with the kite itself. Four line control allows absolutely incredible flying precision even in lower winds. One of the best kite types to make a show for the crowd as flying one precisely looks eyecatching.

Dual-line Kite Flying

Thats probably the first stop for the ones who never flown a kite, but are excited to try. Easy and simple control allows to understand the basics of a kite and wind manipulation. Excellent way for children to accelerate motoric skills development.

Single-line Kite Flying

Single line kites are mostly used as decorations in the sky. Completely effortless flying.They come in various sizes, from a tiny diamond shaped kites that barely stay up in the air, from massive 22m 3d whales and other huge flying objects. All wind powered!
